Atlas of Sign Language Structures



In this area of the platform, you will find linguistic and socio-historical information about the sign languages of the world. These include those sign languages that have been identified and studied so far, but also more under-investigated ones. We know there are many more sign languages in the world that are not sufficiently documented or even not discovered yet.

Under “Atlas by grammatical features”, you find maps that visually represent the grammatical properties of the sign languages of the world. Under “Atlas by sign languages”, you find pages with socio-historical information about each individual sign language.

The data come from questionnaires answered by sign language researchers and users worldwide. The amount and the quality of the information on each sign language and the number of sign languages represented in the Atlas depended on the availability of information and content-providers for each sign language at the time of creating the Atlas. Thus, the number of grammatical features you will find for a certain sign language gives a clue about the current status of its investigation. The answers for some grammatical features are not based on a documented analysis, but rather on a personal estimation. We have indicated personal estimation responses with a small question mark next to the sign languages’ dot. In the future, we would like to extend the shared knowledge about the sign languages of the world step by step. We want to include as much information for as many sign languages as possible. So, if your sign language is not represented here yet, please contact us.
